FAQs
What is the dress code?

Attendees should dress appropriately when the conference is in session. Men should be in suits with jackets and ties and remain in the jacket while speaking. Women should be in dresses and skirts of knee-length/smart pants. Footwear should also match the occasion - no sneakers will be permitted. Shirts should be appropriate and formal - sleeves should extend halfway through the upper arm. No sheer/cut-out shirts will be permitted. No floral prints or bright colors; sticking to neutral tones is recommended.

Furthermore, national clothing is not permitted as it is not appropriate conference attire since most delegates will be representing different countries. Additionally, wearing a delegations propaganda is also not acceptable.

Note: In the case of a massive violation of the dress code, a delegate can be immediately clipped.

Are position papers mandatory?
Yes, all delegates are required to write position papers if they wish to be eligible for an award.
Will there be delegate training sessions?
Yes, 2 delegate training sessions will be conducted for all delegates if they wish to join, particularly first-time attendees.
Am I eligible for an award if I receive a warning?
Yes, you will still be eligible for an award. Before a clipping, in cases of misbehavior, breaking ROP, and unparliamentary language, delegates are given 3 formal warnings. After the first clipping, the delegate will no longer be eligible for an award. After the third clipping, delegates will be forced to leave the conference and not return. A delegate can be immediately clipped in the case of a massive violation of the dress code, carrying an impermissible item, or any physical violence.
