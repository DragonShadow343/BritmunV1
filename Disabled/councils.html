Rules of Procedure

BRITMUN considers that the best approach for students to learn about the United Nations is through the Rules of Procedure, which entails a clear understanding of the benefits and drawbacks of debating conflicts in accordance with these standards is conveyed through a realistic simulation of the subjects discussed using the actual regulations that regulate various UN entities.
Rules of Procedure

Delegate Allocations

Delegate Allocations will provide each delegate (depending on the council) with a country/personality, which they must represent and portray to the best of their ability. The delegate must put aside their personal viewpoints, and embrace that of their assigned country/personality. They must represent their given stance while: debating, writing resolutions, and sending amendments, to better the situation.
